Syntax and Semantics

Peter Norvig, in a blog post [1], explains syntax and semantics as follows. “The syntax of a language is the arrangement of characters to form correct statements or expressions; the semantics is the meaning of those statements or expressions.” Syntax and semantics are not unique concepts to programming, In-fact they are used in formal logic. Peter Smith [2] describes semantics as meaning and truth. He describes syntax as the grammar of logic-based languages: “The syntax of a language tells us, which strings of symbols count as grammatical sentences” [2], p.72.
